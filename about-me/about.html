Nick's Story So Far...

Introduction to Computing

My passion for computing began as a middle schooler's dream to play more modular video games that could be expanded on and modified in ways that are only limited by a game's community and the hardware it runs on. After building my first computer in the 8th grade, solving technical problems became a way of life. There were days that I would spend almost the entirety of sifting through forum posts for similar problems or looking up what the purpose was for an advanced setting in an application in order to get it running again or because it wasn't running smoothly. These experiences became the headstart I needed when I began working as a technician for Repair Authority, which is now the largest home medical equipment repair provider in the U.S.

Learning Workplace Maturity

Repair technician work seriously honed my troubleshooting and diagnostic skills. Every day I worked with skilled individuals that offered their techniques to those who were serious about improvement, and seeing other technician's do the impossible, like conquering 1 hour repair jobs in 25 minutes, was an incredible source of motivation. While I did eventually become a very capable technician, I also learned the importance of work ethic and community; learning to expertly and efficiently handle high skill ceiling, niche work such as medical equipment repair would have never been possible if it weren't for the people around me that valued their work and their willingness to welcome others with the same values. After about 2 years, I knew my work as a medical equipment repair technician could only bring me so far, as my true ambition is to work in either software development or IT.

Path to Professionalism

I started my computer science degree journey at Cuyahoga Community College, where I took my general education courses and a few programming courses. At Cleveland State University, I learned far more about computing, and the advanced topics gave me the foundation and confidence needed to handle difficult areas of the industry, such as abstraction hierarchies in computer architecture, low-level and systems programming, etc.

Career Aspiration in Focus

Now, my goal is to work for a well-coordinated team that shares my strong values of commitment, shared-growth, and community.
