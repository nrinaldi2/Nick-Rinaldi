Projects

FinanceFX

A JavaFX finance overview application I built with my friend Gavin during my first semester at CSU. Between the 3 different menus (Account overview, expenses, calculations), personal finance metrics can be entered and analyzed. Through building FinanceFX, I learned how to process, analyze, and save user input data using an object-oriented design and built-in JavaFX frameworks. Gavin and I also gained valuable experience in dividing up tasks and communicating changes in a cooperative development environment.

NavigationFX

My first GUI application, also using JavaFX, which was developed independently during my last two semesters at Cuyahoga Community College. The user can create a vehicle object, with a name, fuel capacity, and a miles-per-gallon consumption rate. If the vehicle runs out of fuel, after traveling to various (user-defined and pre-set) destinations, they are informed that they are stranded (if they aren't at a destination with a gas station) and a new vehicle must be created to continue. Boundry and robustness testing was a major part of the process in developing NavigationFX. Multiple consecutive days of feeding the application extreme and oddball cases gave me 3 major insights:

  1. No amount of accounting for erroneous input can ever replace the testing process.
  2. Fixing bugs can often create a few more.
  3. Automated testing software is built for a very good reason.
